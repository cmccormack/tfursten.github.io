About Me

I am a Ph.D Candidate in Molecular and Cellular Biology working in the Cartwright lab in the The Biodesign Institute Center for Evolutionary Medicine and Informatics at Arizona State University. I am interested in developing computer simulations and algorithms to model and study various evolutionary and population genetic processes. I am currently working on models to investigate the evolution of plant self-incompatibility systems in the prevention of biparental inbreeding, the effect of different dispersal kernals on isolation-by-distance and estimating neighborhood size using composite marginal likelihoods.
